Does it survive a camera?

First evaluation on the north-star benchmark: 14 physical kids’ puzzles, 236 pieces photographed on four backgrounds — 3,776 samples through the exact deployed preview pipeline. The classical SIFT→NCC hybrid nearly holds its synthetic score. The trained CNN collapses.

Synthetic benchmark vs. real photos

Both-correct accuracy per method. Hollow = synthetic 4×4 benchmark (exp20 re-eval / exp23); filled = north-star real photos.

The two pixel-level methods tell the story. NCC — literal template correlation — loses 28 points to camera photometry and scale error even with a scale search. The CNN, trained on pixel-exact synthetic crops, loses 57: what it learned was pixel-identity, not visual matching. SIFT is the outlier that improves on real photos: its synthetic weakness was coverage on small low-texture crops, and real piece photos are larger and richer in texture. Test-time rotation search (critical-review item #7) recovers only 3.2 points for the CNN — the failure is in the features, not the inference protocol.

Where it breaks

Wood is the hardest background for the hybrid (69.0% vs ~80% elsewhere): grain texture and low piece-to-background contrast leak into segmentation and matching. The CNN is uniformly poor everywhere — its failure is not background-driven.

Eight of fourteen puzzles land at 75–100% for the hybrid. The two weak ones — frozen scene (44%) and unicorn pink (55%) — are low-texture, repetitive artwork where SIFT fails and the weak NCC fallback takes over. That fallback is where the headroom is.

The hybrid’s confusion is a clean diagonal (88–90% per rotation). The CNN’s is diffuse — not even the structured 180° ambiguity it shows on synthetic data — confirming its features barely transfer.

Protocol

Dataset defect found and fixed along the way. The overview JPEGs’ raw pixel orientation was arbitrary — stale EXIF from the HEIC conversion, and EXIF itself was wrong for 4 of 14 puzzles (iPhone orientation is unreliable for top-down shots). Orientation is now measured: the verified-upright piece crops are SIFT-matched against the overview at all four rotations, and the rotation where pieces match with zero residual wins — unanimous 180° for all 14 puzzles (24–94 votes for, ≤8 against). ingest.py now bakes the measured rotation into the pixels.

Conclusions

  1. The critical review’s headline fear is confirmed, quantitatively. The CNN learned pixel-identical matching that does not survive a camera: 72.2% → 14.8% both-correct. Every synthetic result in exp1–exp20 said nothing about the real task.
  2. Classical matching survives. The zero-training hybrid drops only 5.5 points and leads the real benchmark by 28. On real photos it beats the model family the backend currently serves by roughly 5× — shipping it in the backend is a live option today.
  3. The real-task bar is now 76.7% both-correct on north_star v1. Exp26 — training with realism (independent photometric jitter, scale/perspective, real backgrounds, sensor noise) — must beat that number, selected on the frozen-split validation set and touching north-star once.

Next

Also worth doing: ship the SIFT→NCC hybrid behind the piece endpoint meanwhile, and move to a dense heatmap head (item #6) so one model covers 2×3 through 4×6 grids — and eventually 500-piece puzzles.
